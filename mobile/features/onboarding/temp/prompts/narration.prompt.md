# Narration Prompt

## Performance Direction

Read as one continuous performance.

The voice should feel ancient, intimate, warm, close, and certain. It should not sound like a product ad, trailer, or meditation app. The pauses matter as much as the words.

Do not rush. Let the images breathe.

## Spoken Script

```text
Before memory had words,
it had taste.

Before recipes,
there was flame.

Before maps,
there was smoke.

Before names,
there were hands.

Hands that washed.
Hands that cut.
Hands that stirred
until hunger became home.

A people could live
inside a pot.

A place could rise
from a smell.

A voice could return
through a bite.

Someone fed you.

And before you knew
what love was called,
your body knew
what love felt like.

One taste opens the door.
One breath brings back the room.
One bite returns the hands
that held you.

Brioela begins where language ends:
in the taste your body remembers
before your mind does.
```

## Silent Final Text

This line is currently intended to appear on screen after the camera reveal, not be spoken:

```text
Show Brioela something you eat.
```
