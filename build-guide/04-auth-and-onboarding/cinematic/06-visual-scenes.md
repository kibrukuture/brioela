# 06 - Visual Scenes

## Goal

The world should feel like a place between a kitchen, a dream, and the beginning of civilization.

## Visual Language

- blackness
- ember
- smoke
- firelight
- grains
- oil droplets
- spice dust
- steam
- hand shadows
- clay texture
- pot silhouette
- table fragments
- warm distortion
- memory particles

## Main Rule

Everything is half-seen.

Memory is not a full render. Memory is warmth, shape, shadow, and missing detail.

## Scene Sequence

1. Ember appears in black.
2. Flame blooms.
3. Smoke becomes paths and dissolves.
4. Hand shadows enter.
5. Water/cut/stir gestures form the pot spiral.
6. Pot becomes a small universe.
7. Smoke forms room fragments.
8. Bite particle hits the lens and bursts.
9. Particles return inward.
10. Body-memory pulse forms.
11. Wordmark becomes door/lens.
12. Brioela wordmark completes.
13. Camera appears beneath dissolving particles.

## Rule

No decorative effects. Every visual must mean something.

## Detailed Scene Design

### Scene 1 — Before Memory

Narration:

```text
Before memory had words,
it had taste.
```

Visual:

- Pure black.
- One tiny ember appears, barely alive.
- The ember breathes like something waking up.
- Amber light softly touches the screen edges.
- No ingredients yet.

Meaning:

- Memory begins before language.
- The ember is the first food memory.

### Scene 2 — Fire Before Recipe

Narration:

```text
Before recipes,
there was flame.
```

Visual:

- Ember becomes a low cooking flame.
- Heat distortion bends the darkness.
- Ash and spark particles drift upward.
- First true warmth enters the screen.

Meaning:

- Before instruction, there was practice.
- Before data, there was fire.

### Scene 3 — Smoke Before Map

Narration:

```text
Before maps,
there was smoke.
```

Visual:

- Smoke rises and curls.
- It briefly suggests paths, valleys, rivers, migration lines.
- It dissolves before becoming literal.
- Tiny spice and grain particles move through the smoke like people moving through history.

Meaning:

- Food carried place before maps.
- Smoke is culture moving.

### Scene 4 — Hands Before Names

Narration:

```text
Before names,
there were hands.
```

Visual:

- Hand shadows appear in firelight.
- No detailed hands, no literal person.
- Show motion: washing, cutting, stirring.
- Each gesture disturbs the particle world.
- Ingredients appear because hands have entered.

Meaning:

- Before identity, there was care through action.
- Hands are the first technology.

### Scene 5 — The Work Of Care

Narration:

```text
Hands that washed.
Hands that cut.
Hands that stirred
until hunger became home.
```

Visual:

- Washed: water shimmer through grains.
- Cut: clean line of light through darkness.
- Stirred: pot-shaped spiral of particles.
- Home: spiral becomes warm and stable.

Meaning:

- Cooking turns survival into belonging.

### Scene 6 — Culture In A Pot

Narration:

```text
A people could live
inside a pot.
```

Visual:

- Pot silhouette becomes the whole universe.
- Inside: moving lights, grains, steam, spice, oil.
- No literal people.
- Patterns may suggest textile, rhythm, ceremony, or migration for one breath.

Meaning:

- Culture is stored in food.
- A dish can carry an entire people.

### Scene 7 — Place From Smell

Narration:

```text
A place could rise
from a smell.
```

Visual:

- Smoke forms room fragments: doorway edge, table line, window light, steam.
- The user should feel the room, not see the room.
- Phone tilt shifts fragments with depth.

Meaning:

- Smell recreates place instantly.

### Scene 8 — Voice Through Bite

Narration:

```text
A voice could return
through a bite.
```

Visual:

- Everything goes almost still.
- One food particle/droplet/grain moves toward camera.
- On “bite,” it touches the lens and the memory world bursts outward.

Meaning:

- Taste can return presence.
- Not death-focused; presence-focused.

### Scene 9 — The Personal Collapse

Narration:

```text
Someone fed you.
```

Visual:

- After burst, particles are far away.
- They begin returning inward.
- Civilization-scale story becomes personal.
- No face. No specific person. Just warmth.

### Scene 10 — Love Before Language

Narration:

```text
And before you knew
what love was called,
your body knew
what love felt like.
```

Visual:

- Returning particles form a pulse/body warmth.
- A soft heartbeat ring appears.
- Ring begins becoming letterforms.

Meaning:

- Being fed is pre-verbal love.

### Scene 11 — The Door

Narration:

```text
One taste opens the door.
One breath brings back the room.
One bite returns the hands
that held you.
```

Visual:

- Wordmark behaves like door/lens.
- Door: thin opening of light.
- Room: room fragments return behind logo.
- Hands: hand-shadow flickers once.
- Held you: frame edges warm like an embrace.

Do not show arms or hugging.

### Scene 12 — Brioela Arrives

Narration:

```text
Brioela begins where language ends:
in the taste your body remembers
before your mind does.
```

Visual:

- Brioela wordmark completes.
- It arrives like truth, not a superhero trailer.
- Particles lock softly but with weight.
- One low cinematic note and haptic land.
- Wordmark breathes once.

### Scene 13 — Camera Becomes The World

On-screen text only:

```text
Show Brioela something you eat.
```

Visual:

- Wordmark becomes a lens.
- Lens opens into camera.
- Particles fade over real camera feed.
- No button, carousel, or “get started.”
- Final text appears small, bottom-center.

Meaning:

- The myth enters reality.
- The user’s actual food becomes part of the story.

## Color Palette

- black
- ember orange
- honey gold
- smoke gray
- clay brown
- cream
- tiny red sparks
- final camera natural colors

Avoid rainbow color, bright app UI colors, green health-app palette, and blue tech glow.

## Texture Direction

- film grain
- smoke softness
- oil shimmer
- ash specks
- clay roughness
- steam blur
- heat distortion
- tiny light leaks

The world should feel warm, edible, old, and alive.
