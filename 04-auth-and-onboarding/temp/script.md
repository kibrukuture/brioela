# Brioela Onboarding Script - Temporary Working File

This is a temporary workspace for the cinematic Brioela onboarding script. Move it later when the onboarding design is finalized.

## Purpose

The onboarding is not a tutorial, carousel, feature list, dashboard intro, or product explanation.

It should feel like a short cinematic opening: a mythic, intimate first chapter where the user feels what Brioela is before they are asked to do anything.

The goal is to make the user feel:

- food is older than language
- taste and smell carry memory before thought
- being fed is one of the first forms of love
- food carries people, places, culture, and civilization
- Brioela begins in that pre-verbal layer
- the camera reveal is not a button; it is the story entering the user's real world

## Rules We Found

- Do not explain features.
- Do not say calories, macros, labels, price, scanning, tracking, or dashboard.
- Do not make it sound like a nutrition app.
- Do not make it feel like an ad.
- Do not over-focus on grandma or death; those are powerful, but too narrow if stated directly.
- Use universal human psychology: taste, smell, hands, fire, home, memory, being fed.
- Move from civilization to intimacy.
- Make it rhythmic like music, not childish rhyme.
- Use short lines that a cinematic narrator can breathe through.
- Let visuals carry much of the meaning.
- Use the line: "Brioela begins where language ends: in the taste your body remembers before your mind does."

## Psychological Foundation

These are the truths the script must reach without explaining them directly:

- Smell and taste reach memory before conscious thought.
- Every person has at least one food that takes them somewhere instantly.
- The people who fed us were often the first people who loved us.
- Food is culture and civilization carried through hands, fire, recipes, pots, tables, and memory.
- Food can make absent people and places feel present again.

## Tone

The voice should feel:

- ancient
- intimate
- warm
- certain
- cinematic
- sacred, not epic
- like a story told close to the ear

Avoid a trailer voice that feels like a product launch. The better reference is a myth, folktale, or opening narration that feels personal to one listener.

## Current Best Script

```text
Before memory had words,
it had taste.

Before recipes,
there was flame.

Before maps,
there was smoke.

Before names,
there were hands.

Hands that washed.
Hands that cut.
Hands that stirred
until hunger became home.

A people could live
inside a pot.

A place could rise
from a smell.

A voice could return
through a bite.

Someone fed you.

And before you knew
what love was called,
your body knew
what love felt like.

One taste opens the door.
One breath brings back the room.
One bite returns the hands
that held you.

Brioela begins where language ends:
in the taste your body remembers
before your mind does.

Show Brioela something you eat.
```

## Why This Version Works

- It starts ancient: memory before words.
- It includes civilization without saying "civilization."
- It uses elemental images: flame, smoke, hands, pot, smell, bite.
- It moves from humanity to one person.
- It creates rhythm through repeated structures: "Before...", "Hands...", "One..."
- It gives culture a place: "A people could live inside a pot."
- It gives memory a place: "A place could rise from a smell."
- It gives absence a place: "A voice could return through a bite."
- It lands on the strongest thesis line near the end.
- It ends in the camera action without sounding like a product instruction.

## Visual Arc To Explore Later

Do not design the animation before the script is settled. But the current script suggests this visual path:

1. Black screen, ember or smoke.
2. Flame appears on "Before recipes, there was flame."
3. Smoke becomes a moving memory field.
4. Hands appear abstractly through light, shadow, or particle silhouettes.
5. A pot becomes a small universe of food particles and culture fragments.
6. Smell becomes visible as warm vapor that forms a place.
7. A voice/person is implied for one breath, not shown literally.
8. Particles gather into the Brioela wordmark on the thesis line.
9. Wordmark becomes a lens.
10. Lens opens into camera.

## Open Script Questions

- Is "A voice could return through a bite" too direct, or exactly right?
- Should the final line be "Show Brioela something you eat" or "Show it something you eat"?
- Should "flame" be "fire" for simpler universality?
- Does "A people could live inside a pot" land globally, or does it need a more grounded image?
- Should the narration be closer to 20 seconds or 32 seconds?
