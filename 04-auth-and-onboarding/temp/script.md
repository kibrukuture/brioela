# Brioela Onboarding Script - Temporary Working File

This is a temporary workspace for the cinematic Brioela onboarding script. Move it later when the onboarding design is finalized.

## Purpose

The onboarding is not a tutorial, carousel, feature list, dashboard intro, or product explanation.

It should feel like a short cinematic opening: a mythic, intimate first chapter where the user feels what Brioela is before they are asked to do anything.

The goal is to make the user feel:

- food is older than language
- taste and smell carry memory before thought
- being fed is one of the first forms of love
- food carries people, places, culture, and civilization
- Brioela begins in that pre-verbal layer
- the camera reveal is not a button; it is the story entering the user's real world

## Rules We Found

- Do not explain features.
- Do not say calories, macros, labels, price, scanning, tracking, or dashboard.
- Do not make it sound like a nutrition app.
- Do not make it feel like an ad.
- Do not over-focus on grandma or death; those are powerful, but too narrow if stated directly.
- Use universal human psychology: taste, smell, hands, fire, home, memory, being fed.
- Move from civilization to intimacy.
- Make it rhythmic like music, not childish rhyme.
- Use short lines that a cinematic narrator can breathe through.
- Let visuals carry much of the meaning.
- Use the line: "Brioela begins where language ends: in the taste your body remembers before your mind does."

## Psychological Foundation

These are the truths the script must reach without explaining them directly:

- Smell and taste reach memory before conscious thought.
- Every person has at least one food that takes them somewhere instantly.
- The people who fed us were often the first people who loved us.
- Food is culture and civilization carried through hands, fire, recipes, pots, tables, and memory.
- Food can make absent people and places feel present again.

## Tone

The voice should feel:

- ancient
- intimate
- warm
- certain
- cinematic
- sacred, not epic
- like a story told close to the ear

Avoid a trailer voice that feels like a product launch. The better reference is a myth, folktale, or opening narration that feels personal to one listener.

## Why This Version Works

- It starts ancient: memory before words.
- It includes civilization without saying "civilization."
- It uses elemental images: flame, smoke, hands, pot, smell, bite.
- It moves from humanity to one person.
- It creates rhythm through repeated structures: "Before...", "Hands...", "One..."
- It gives culture a place: "A people could live inside a pot."
- It gives memory a place: "A place could rise from a smell."
- It gives absence a place: "A voice could return through a bite."
- It lands on the strongest thesis line near the end.
- It ends in the camera action without sounding like a product instruction.

## Visual Arc To Explore Later

Do not design the animation before the script is settled. But the current script suggests this visual path:

1. Black screen, ember or smoke.
2. Flame appears on "Before recipes, there was flame."
3. Smoke becomes a moving memory field.
4. Hands appear abstractly through light, shadow, or particle silhouettes.
5. A pot becomes a small universe of food particles and culture fragments.
6. Smell becomes visible as warm vapor that forms a place.
7. A voice/person is implied for one breath, not shown literally.
8. Particles gather into the Brioela wordmark on the thesis line.
9. Wordmark becomes a lens.
10. Lens opens into camera.

- Does "A people could live inside a pot" land globally, or does it need a more grounded image?

## Cinematic Scenery Direction - Current Favorite

The scenery should feel like a place between a kitchen, a dream, and the beginning of civilization.

It should not look like:

- a realistic kitchen
- a fantasy cartoon
- a food commercial
- a UI animation
- a feature explainer

It should feel like the user is inside the first memory of food.

The world is built from:

- blackness
- ember
- smoke
- firelight
- floating grains
- oil droplets
- spice dust
- steam
- hand shadows
- clay texture
- pot silhouette
- table fragments
- warm air distortion
- memory particles

No modern objects. No labels. No barcodes. No UI cards. No literal restaurant. No literal grandma. No obvious house.

### Main Visual Rule

Everything should be half-seen.

The user should feel: "I know this place, but I cannot fully see it."

That is memory.

- Hands are shadows, not full hands.
- A table is an edge of light, not a table model.
- A pot is a silhouette, not a rendered object.
- A person is a presence, not a face.
- A room is warmth and shape, not walls.

This keeps it universal. If the scene shows a specific grandmother, country, family, or kitchen, it becomes smaller. If it shows smoke, hands, fire, and a bowl, every person can fill in their own memory.

## Detailed Scene Design

### Scene 1 - Before Memory

Narration:

```text
Before memory had words,
it had taste.
```

Visual:

- Pure black.
- No logo.
- No text at first.
- One tiny ember appears, barely alive.
- The ember does not ignite like a firework.
- It breathes like something waking up.
- Amber light softly touches the edges of the screen.

Meaning:

- Memory begins before language.
- The ember is the first food memory.

Implementation note:

- Do not show ingredients yet. It is too early.

### Scene 2 - Fire Before Recipe

Narration:

```text
Before recipes,
there was flame.
```

Visual:

- Ember becomes flame.
- The flame is low, intimate, and sacred.
- It should feel like a cooking flame, not a bonfire.
- Heat distortion bends the darkness around it.
- Ash and spark particles drift upward.
- The first true warmth enters the screen.

Meaning:

- Before instruction, there was practice.
- Before data, there was fire.

Timing:

- First important haptic lands here, on "flame".

### Scene 3 - Smoke Before Map

Narration:

```text
Before maps,
there was smoke.
```

Visual:

- Smoke rises and curls.
- The smoke starts behaving like geography.
- It briefly suggests paths, valleys, rivers, migration lines.
- It dissolves before it becomes literal.
- Tiny spice and grain particles travel through the smoke like people moving through history.

Meaning:

- Food is how people carried place before maps.
- Smoke is culture moving.
- Civilization enters without saying civilization.

### Scene 4 - Hands Before Names

Narration:

```text
Before names,
there were hands.
```

Visual:

- Shadows of hands appear in the firelight.
- Do not show detailed hands.
- Show motion: washing, cutting, stirring.
- Each gesture disturbs the particle world.
- Ingredients appear because hands have entered.

Meaning:

- Before identity, before names, there was care through action.
- Hands are the first technology.

Direction:

- This should feel holy, not sentimental.

### Scene 5 - The Work Of Care

Narration:

```text
Hands that washed.
Hands that cut.
Hands that stirred
until hunger became home.
```

Visual:

- "Washed" creates a water shimmer through grains.
- "Cut" creates a clean line of light through darkness.
- "Stirred" begins a pot-shaped spiral of particles.
- On "until hunger became home," the spiral becomes warm and stable.
- Everything slows down.

Meaning:

- Cooking turns survival into belonging.
- Hunger becomes home because someone cared enough to transform it.

Timing:

- Long pause after "home".
- Let the screen breathe.
- No extra movement. Just fire and the pot-universe.

### Scene 6 - Culture In A Pot

Narration:

```text
A people could live
inside a pot.
```

Visual:

- The pot silhouette becomes the whole universe.
- Inside it: moving lights, grains, steam, spice, oil.
- Do not show literal people.
- A whole culture is implied through motion and pattern.
- For one breath, patterns may resemble textile, rhythm, ceremony, or migration, then dissolve.

Meaning:

- Culture is stored in food.
- A dish can carry an entire people.

Direction:

- This may be the strongest visual line. Do not rush it.

### Scene 7 - Place From Smell

Narration:

```text
A place could rise
from a smell.
```

Visual:

- Smoke rises from the pot.
- It forms fragments of a room:
  - doorway edge
  - table line
  - window light
  - steam crossing the air
- The scene should look like a memory reconstructing itself.
- The user should feel the room, not see the room.

Meaning:

- Smell recreates place instantly.

Interaction:

- This is where gyroscope/parallax can become strongest.
- Tilt the phone and the room fragments shift like they have depth.

### Scene 8 - Voice Through Bite

Narration:

```text
A voice could return
through a bite.
```

Visual:

- Everything goes almost still.
- A single food particle, droplet, or grain moves toward the camera.
- On "bite," it touches the lens.
- The memory world bursts outward.

Meaning:

- One taste brings someone or somewhere back.
- This is not about death. It is about presence.

Timing:

- Burst, whoosh, and haptic land on "bite".
- The burst should not feel like spectacle. It should feel like memory hitting the body.

### Scene 9 - The Personal Collapse

Narration:

```text
Someone fed you.
```

Visual:

- After the burst, all particles are far away.
- On this line, particles begin returning inward.
- The world stops being civilization-scale and becomes personal.
- No face. No specific person.
- Just warmth gathering back toward the center.

Meaning:

- The huge human story collapses into one human act.

Timing:

- This line needs silence around it.

### Scene 10 - Love Before Language

Narration:

```text
And before you knew
what love was called,
your body knew
what love felt like.
```

Visual:

- Returning particles assemble slowly.
- First they form a pulse or body-like warmth.
- A soft ring appears, like a heartbeat.
- The ring begins becoming letterforms.

Meaning:

- Being fed is pre-verbal love.
- The body knew before the mind knew.

Direction:

- This is the emotional peak before Brioela appears.

### Scene 11 - The Door

Narration:

```text
One taste opens the door.
One breath brings back the room.
One bite returns the hands
that held you.
```

Visual:

- The forming wordmark also behaves like a door or lens.
- "Door" creates a thin opening of light.
- "Room" brings back the room fragments behind the logo.
- "Hands" makes the hand-shadow flicker once more.
- "Held you" warms the frame edges like an embrace.

Meaning:

- Taste is a portal.
- The past returns through the body.

Direction:

- Do not show arms or hugging.
- Keep "held you" subtle. Warmth around the frame is enough.

### Scene 12 - Brioela Arrives

Narration:

```text
Brioela begins where language ends:
in the taste your body remembers
before your mind does.
```

Visual:

- Brioela wordmark completes.
- It should not slam in like a superhero trailer.
- It should arrive like truth.
- Particles lock into place softly but with weight.
- One low cinematic note hits.
- Phone haptic lands.
- The wordmark breathes once.

Meaning:

- Brioela is not introduced as a product.
- Brioela is introduced as the keeper of this pre-verbal food-memory layer.

Direction:

- This is where the strongest thesis line belongs.
- It should feel inevitable.

### Scene 13 - Camera Becomes The World

On-screen text only:

```text
Show Brioela something you eat.
```

Visual:

- Wordmark becomes a lens.
- The lens opens into camera.
- Particles fade over the real camera feed.
- The user's real room appears.
- No button.
- No carousel.
- No "get started".
- Final text appears small, bottom-center.

Meaning:

- The myth enters reality.
- The user's actual food becomes part of the story.

Audio:

- The narrator should stop before the camera fully opens.
- The final line is silent text over the real world.

## Strongest Cinematic Beats

Do not make something happen on every line. Cinematic pacing is not line/effect/line/effect. It is tension, stillness, event, silence, release.

The strongest moments are:

1. Flame appears.
2. Hands appear.
3. "Until hunger became home."
4. "A people could live inside a pot."
5. "A voice could return through a bite." Burst.
6. "Someone fed you." Convergence begins.
7. Brioela thesis line. Wordmark completes.
8. Camera reveal.

Everything else should support these moments.

## Color Palette

Use very few colors:

- black
- ember orange
- honey gold
- smoke gray
- clay brown
- cream
- tiny red sparks
- final camera natural colors

Avoid:

- rainbow color
- bright app UI colors
- green health-app palette
- blue tech glow

Brioela should feel warm, edible, old, and alive.

## Texture Direction

Texture matters more than shape.

Use:

- film grain
- smoke softness
- oil shimmer
- ash specks
- clay roughness
- steam blur
- heat distortion
- tiny light leaks

This is what makes the world cinematic instead of decorative.
